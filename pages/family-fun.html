Family Fun in Germany — Adventures for All Ages

Germany is a playground for families! From world-class theme parks to interactive museums and fairy tale forests, create happy memories together in 2026.

Theme Parks & Adventure

Germany hosts some of Europe's best theme parks, offering thrills and fun for toddlers to teens.

  • Europa-Park (Rust): Germany's largest theme park with European-themed areas and over 100 attractions.
  • Playmobil FunPark (Zirndorf): A giant playground where kids can interact with life-sized Playmobil sets.
  • Phantasialand (Brühl): Known for its immersive worlds and award-winning roller coasters.

Miniatur Wunderland (Hamburg)

The world's largest model railway is a must-see. Watch tiny trains, planes, and ships move through detailed landscapes of Germany, Scandinavia, and the Americas.

The Fairy Tale Route

Follow the footsteps of the Brothers Grimm on this magical route from Hanau to Bremen.

  • Highlights: Visit Sleeping Beauty's castle (Sababurg) and the Pied Piper's town (Hamelin).
  • Activity: Look out for open-air theater performances in summer.

Interactive Museums

Make learning fun at these hands-on science centers.

  • Deutsches Museum (Munich): The world's largest museum of science and technology.
  • Universum (Bremen): Explore the themes of mankind, earth, and the cosmos in a futuristic building.
  • Klimahaus (Bremerhaven): Travel through the world's climate zones in one day.

Nature for Kids

Germany's outdoors are perfect for little explorers.

  • Treetop Walks (Baumwipfelpfade): Walk above the forest canopy in the Black Forest or Bavarian Forest.
  • Barefoot Parks: Sensory experiences walking on different textures like mud, wood, and stone.

Family Travel FAQs

Are trains in Germany stroller-friendly?

Yes, most ICE and regional trains have dedicated spaces for strollers and family compartments. It's best to reserve seats in the family area.

Do kids travel for free?

Children under 6 travel free on Deutsche Bahn. Children aged 6-14 travel free if accompanied by parents/grandparents, but they must be entered on the ticket.

Are restaurants kid-friendly?

Generally yes. Many offer "Kinderteller" (children's plates) and high chairs. It's common to see families dining out.
