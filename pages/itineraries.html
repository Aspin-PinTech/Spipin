Dreamy Itineraries — Explore Germany

Planning a trip can be overwhelming. We've curated sustainable, rail-friendly itineraries to help you see the best of Germany in 2026.

1. Classic Germany (10 Days)

A perfect introduction to Germany, covering history, nature, and modern culture. Best traveled by train.

Day 1-2: Frankfurt & Heidelberg

  • Arrive: Frankfurt Airport (FRA).
  • Do: Explore the Römerberg and Museumsufer in Frankfurt. Take a short train ride to Heidelberg.
  • Stay: Heidelberg Old Town.

Day 3-4: The Black Forest

  • Travel: Train to Freiburg im Breisgau (approx. 2 hours).
  • Do: Visit the Freiburg Minster, explore the eco-district Vauban, and take a day trip to Lake Titisee.

Day 5-7: Munich & Bavaria

  • Travel: Train to Munich (approx. 4 hours).
  • Do: Marienplatz, English Garden, and a day trip to Neuschwanstein Castle.

Day 8-9: Berlin

  • Travel: ICE Train to Berlin (approx. 4 hours).
  • Do: Brandenburg Gate, Reichstag, and Museum Island.

Day 10: Departure

  • Depart: Berlin Brandenburg Airport (BER) or train to next destination.

2. Northern Highlights (7 Days)

Discover the maritime charm of the Hanseatic cities and the Baltic coast.

Day 1-3: Hamburg

  • Do: Miniatur Wunderland, Elbphilharmonie, and a harbor boat tour.

Day 4: Lübeck

  • Travel: Regional train (45 mins).
  • Do: Holstentor gate and marzipan tasting.

Day 5-7: Rügen Island

  • Travel: Train to Binz (approx. 3 hours).
  • Do: White chalk cliffs (Königsstuhl) and relaxing on the beach.

Budget Travel Tips

Germany can be affordable if you plan ahead. Here is how to save euros while exploring.

  • Eat Street Food: Grab a Bratwurst or Döner Kebab for a cheap, filling meal (approx. 5-7€).
  • Free Museums: Many museums offer free entry on specific days or for students.
  • Drink Tap Water: Bring a reusable bottle. German tap water ("Leitungswasser") is excellent quality.
  • City Cards: Look for "Welcome Cards" in major cities which bundle public transport and attraction discounts.

Travel Tips

  • Rail Pass: Consider a German Rail Pass or the Deutschland-Ticket for regional travel.
  • Luggage: Pack light as you will be moving between trains.
  • Reservations: Book ICE seats in advance, especially in summer.
