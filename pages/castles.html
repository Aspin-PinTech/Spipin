German Castles — Fairytales & History

Germany is famous for its 20,000+ castles, ranging from medieval fortresses to romantic palaces. Here is how to visit them responsibly.

Must-Visit Castles

While there are thousands to choose from, these iconic sites are accessible by public transport.

  • Neuschwanstein Castle: The real-life inspiration for Disney. Located in Bavaria, accessible via train to Füssen and a short bus ride.
  • Burg Eltz: A medieval fairy tale hidden in a forest valley near the Mosel river. Hike in from the nearest train station for a green arrival.
  • Heidelberg Castle: Overlooking the old town, this romantic ruin is easily reached by the funicular railway (Bergbahn).
  • Hohenzollern Castle: Perched atop a mountain, offering spectacular panoramic views of the Swabian Jura.

Sustainable Visiting Tips

Preserving these historic sites requires responsible tourism.

  • Take the Train: Most major castles are connected to the Deutsche Bahn network. Avoid parking hassles and reduce emissions.
  • Stay on Paths: Many castles are surrounded by protected nature reserves. Sticking to marked trails protects local flora.
  • Support Preservation: Entry fees often go directly to the maintenance of these centuries-old structures.

Hidden Gems

Avoid the crowds by visiting lesser-known but equally stunning locations.

  • Lichtenstein Castle: Often called the "fairytale castle of Württemberg," perched on a cliff edge.
  • Cochem Castle: Rising above the Mosel river, surrounded by vineyards.
  • Wartburg Castle: A UNESCO World Heritage site where Martin Luther translated the New Testament.

Stay in a Castle

For a unique experience, consider staying in a "Burghotel". Many historic castles have been converted into sustainable hotels (Jugendherbergen or luxury stays), allowing you to sleep within history.
