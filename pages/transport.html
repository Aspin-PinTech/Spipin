Transportation Guide — Getting Around Sustainably

Germany has one of the best public transportation networks in the world. Whether by high-speed train or bicycle, getting around is easy and eco-friendly.

Train Travel (Deutsche Bahn)

The train is the backbone of sustainable travel in Germany.

  • ICE (InterCity Express): High-speed trains connecting major cities (e.g., Berlin to Munich in under 4 hours).
  • Regional Trains (RE/RB): Connect smaller towns and scenic routes. Slower but perfect for sightseeing.
  • Deutschland-Ticket: For 49€ per month, you can use all regional and local transport across Germany (excludes ICE/IC trains).

Biking in Germany

Germany is a cyclist's paradise with over 70,000 km of long-distance cycle routes.

  • Radwege: Dedicated bike paths are common in cities and countryside.
  • Bike Sharing: Most major cities have app-based bike rentals (e.g., Call a Bike, Nextbike).
  • Taking Bikes on Trains: You can take your bike on most trains, but you often need a separate bike ticket and reservation for long-distance trains.

Public Transit (ÖPNV)

Every city has an integrated system of buses, trams, and trains.

  • U-Bahn: Underground metro systems in large cities like Berlin, Hamburg, Munich.
  • S-Bahn: Suburban trains connecting city centers with outlying areas.
  • Trams (Straßenbahn): Common in many cities, offering a scenic way to get around.

Night Trains

Save on a hotel and wake up in a new destination. The Nightjet network connects German cities with neighbors like Austria, Switzerland, and Italy.

Carbon Offsetting

While trains are low-emission, you can offset unavoidable emissions from flights or car travel through certified projects like Atmosfair. This helps fund renewable energy projects worldwide.
